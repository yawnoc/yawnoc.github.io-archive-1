Vol. II ¶1. Reducing fractions

[▲ Paragraphs] [manuscript] A26 B153(148) C52130(2.1) D39
今有一十八分之一十二。問約之得幾何？
答曰：三分之二。
Now there be twelve eighteenths (12/18). We ask: how much resulteth, upon reducing it?
Answer saith: two thirds (2/3).
  • 一十八分之一十二： twelve eighteenths (12/18); literally twelve (12) of eighteen shares (/18); or twelve (12) of eighteen portions (/18)
術曰：置十八分在下，一十二分在上。副置二位，以少減多，等數得六為法，約之，即得。
Method saith: put the eighteen (18) shares below, and the twelve (12) shares above. Put subsidiarily the two places, and diminish the greater by the lesser. For the equal quantity we obtain six (6) as the divisor, and reducing them, we are done.
  • 副： subsidiarily
  • 位： places

Here we have the subtraction-only version of the Euclidean algorithm for greatest common divisor (gcd):

\begin{aligned} 18 - 12 &= 6 \\ 12 - 6 &= 6 \end{aligned}

Therefore \gcd (12, 18) = 6 and we have

\frac{12}{18} = \frac{12 \div 6}{18 \div 6} = \frac{2}{3}.
  • 以少減多，等數得六： diminish the greater by the lesser. For the equal quantity we obtain six (6)
    In my opinion this description of the algorithm lacks clarity; there isn't any instruction here to repeat the subtraction until you get the equal quantity 6. A better description is found in 《九章算術》, "Nine Chapters on Computational Methods", in the chapter 〈方田〉, 'Rectangular Fields', which has the same problem of reducing 12/18, as well as 49/91. The bracketed portions below are inline two-column annotations by 劉徽 (Lau Fai) (Liu Hui) (Liu Hui), see the 《四部叢刊初編》 version. Punctuation for these bracketed annotations is mine:
    今有十八分之十二。問約之得幾何？
    答曰：三分之二。
    Now there be twelve eighteenths (12/18). We ask: how much resulteth, upon reducing it?
    Answer saith: two thirds (2/3).
    又有九十一分之四十九。問約之得幾何？
    答曰：十三分之七。
    Also there be forty-nine ninety-firsts (49/91). We ask: how much resulteth, upon reducing it?
    Answer saith: seven thirteenths (7/13).
    約分（按：約分者，物之數量不可悉全，必以分言之。分之為數，繁則難用。設有四分之二者。繁而言之，亦可為八分之四；約而言之，則二分之一也。雖則異辭，至於為數，亦同歸爾。法實相推，動有參差，故為術者，先治諸分。）術曰：
    The Method of Reducing Fractions (Note: Reducing Fractions— when a number of objects cannot be completely whole, we must speak of them using fractions. A fraction being a quantity, if complicated, is difficult to utilise. Suppose there be two fourths (2/4). Complicating it in speaking of it, it may also be four eighths (4/8); reducing it in speaking of it, one half (1/2). Although different in their terms, as to the quantities they are, these do return the same value. Divisors and dividends in calculation with each other, have unevenness in their motions; therefore we perform the method, first administering it unto the fractions.) saith:
    • 約： reducing; or simplifying
    • 全： whole; or intact
    • 動有參差： have unevenness in their motions
      This is a literal rendering. I think 劉徽 (Lau Fai) (Liu Hui) (Liu Hui) is trying to say that different representations of fractions lead to inconsistencies (so first reduce them before doing any other calculations).
    • 諸
      Contraction of 之於 (I think).
    可半者半之。不可半者，副置分母、子之數，以少減多，更相減損，求其等也。以等數約之。 （等數約之，即除也。其所以相減者，皆等數之重疊。故以等數約之。）
    those which can be halved: halve them. Those which cannot be halved: put subsidiarily the quantities of the denominator and the numerator of the fraction. Diminish the greater by the lesser, and further subtract them from each other, seeking their being equal. Reduce them using the equal quantity. (To reduce them with the equal quantity is to divide them. Those of them which have been mutually subtracted, are all repeated pilings of the equal quantity. Therefore we use the equal quantity to reduce them.)
    • 重疊： repeated pilings; or overlappings
      I think this means "multiples".

Vol. II ¶2. Adding fractions

[▲ Paragraphs] [manuscript] A26 B153(148) C52133(2.2) D39
今有三分之一、五分之二。問合之二得幾何？
答曰：一十五分之一十一。
Now there be one of three shares (1/3) and two of five shares (2/5). We ask: how much resulteth, upon joining them two?
Answer saith: eleven of fifteen shares (11/15).
  • Version C has only 合之 for 合之二.
  • Version B has 十一 for 一十一.
  • b 分之 a: literally a of b shares
    I have rendered fractions literally from here up to and including ¶4, as there will be references to the numerator 「之 a」, "a of" and the denominator 「b 分」, "b shares" to follow.
術曰：置三分、五分在右方，之一、之二在左方。
Method saith: put the three shares (/3) and the five shares (/5) on the right; the one of (1) and the two of (2), on the left.
母互乘子，五分之二得六，三分之一得五，并之，得一十一為實。右方二母相乘，得一十五為法。
The denominators are mutually multiplied with the numerators: for the two of five shares (2/5) there resulteth six (6), and for the one of three shares (1/3) there resulteth five (5); combining them, resulteth in eleven (11) as the dividend. The two denominators to the right, multiplied with each other, result in fifteen (15) as the divisor.

In modern notation:

\frac{1}{3} + \frac{2}{5} = \frac{1 \times 5 + 2 \times 3}{3 \times 5} = \frac{11}{15}
不滿法，以法命之，即得。
The dividend reacheth not the divisor; naming it for a fraction using the divisor, we are done.
  • 不滿法： The dividend reacheth not the divisor
    This is saying that 11 < 15, i.e. the fraction 11/15 is proper (so no further division is necessary).

Vol. II ¶3. Subtracting fractions

[▲ Paragraphs] [manuscript] A28 B153(148) C52136(2.3) D40
今有九分之八，減其五分之一。問餘幾何？
答曰：四十五分之三十一。
Now there be eight of nine shares (8/9), and we diminish of it one of five shares (1/5). We ask: how much remaineth?
Answer saith: thirty-one of forty-five shares (31/45).
術曰：置九分、五分在右方，之八、之一在左方。
Method saith: put the nine shares (/9) and the five shares (/5) on the right; the eight of (8) and the one of (1), on the left.
母互乘子，五分之一得九，九分之八得四十，以少減多，餘三十一為實。母相乘得四十五，為法。
The denominators are mutually multiplied with the numerators: for the one of five shares (1/5) there resulteth nine (9), and for the eight of nine shares (8/9) there resulteth forty (40); diminishing the greater by the lesser, there remaineth thirty-one (31) as the dividend. The denominators multiplied with each other, result in forty-five (45) as the divisor.
  • Version C erroneously has 王十一 for 三十一.
\frac{8}{9} - \frac{1}{5} = \frac{8 \times 5 - 1 \times 9}{9 \times 5} = \frac{31}{45}
不滿法，以法命之，即得。
The dividend reacheth not the divisor; naming it for a fraction using the divisor, we are done.

Vol. II ¶4. Redistributing fractions for equality

[▲ Paragraphs] [manuscript] A28 B153(148) C52139(2.4) D41
今有三分之一、三分之二、四分之三。問減多益少，幾何而平？
Now there be one of three shares (1/3), two of three shares (2/3) and three of four shares (3/4). We ask: how much shall we diminish the greater to benefit the lesser, that they be level?
  • 而平： that they be level; or that it be fair
答曰：減四分之三者二，三分之二者一，并以益三分之一，而各平於一十二分之七。
Answer saith: that diminished of the three of four shares (3/4), is two (2); that of the two of three shares (2/3), one (1); combined to benefit the one of three shares (1/3), that each be level at seven of twelve shares (7/12).
\frac{3}{4} - {\color{red} \frac{2}{12}} = \frac{2}{3} - {\color{darkviolet} \frac{1}{12}} = \frac{1}{3} + \left( \color{green} \frac{2}{12} + \frac{1}{12} \right) = {\color{blue} \frac{7}{12}}

More generally, consider redistributing the fractions a/A, b/B and c/C for equality. Their mean is

m = \frac{L}{3 ABC},

where

L = aBC + bCA + cAB,

so the fractions must be changed by the quantities

m - \left( \frac{a}{A}, \frac{b}{B}, \frac{c}{C} \right) = \frac{L - (3 aBC, 3 bCA, 3 cAB)}{3 ABC}

respectively. Of course Sun Tzŭ did not have the luxury of modern mathematical notation:

術曰：置三分、三分、四分在右方，之一、之二、之三在左方。母互乘子，副并得六十三，置右為平實。
Method saith: put the three shares (/3), the three shares (/3) and the four shares (/4) on the right; the one of (1), the two of (2) and the three of (3), on the left. The denominators, mutually multiplied with the numerators, combined subsidiarily, result in sixty-three (63), put to the right as the level dividend.
  • Version C erroneously has 母互乘于 for 母互乘子.
  • 平實： level dividend
    L = aBC + bCA + cAB = 63.
\begin{alignedat}{3.5} a &= 1 \qquad & A &= 3 \qquad & aBC &= 12 \\ b &= 2 \qquad & B &= 3 \qquad & bCA &= 24 \\ c &= 3 \qquad & C &= 4 \qquad & cAB &= 27 \\ \hline & & & & L &= 63 \end{alignedat}
母相乘，得三十六為法。
The denominators multiplied with each other, result in thirty-six (36) as the divisor.
ABC = 3 \times 3 \times 4 = 36
以列數三乘未并者及法。
Multiply those not yet combined, and the divisor, by the number of rows, three (3).
  • 未并者： those not yet combined
    These are the individual terms aBC, bCA and cAB, before they were combined subsidiarily to make the level dividend L.
\begin{aligned} 3 aBC &= 36 \\ 3 bCA &= 72 \\ 3 cAB &= 81 \\ 3 ABC &= 108 \end{aligned}
等數得九，約訖。
For their equal quantity there resulteth nine (9) . Finish reducing them.
  • 等數： equal quantity
    Greatest common divisor (gcd) is called an "equal quantity" because it is determined by repeated subtraction until an equal quantity is obtained, see ¶1.
\gcd (L, 3 aBC, 3 bCA, 3 cAB, 3 ABC) = 9

Therefore

\begin{aligned} \frac{L - (3 aBC, 3 bCA, 3 cAB)}{3 ABC} &= \frac{63 \div 9 - (36, 72, 81) \div 9}{108 \div 9} \\[2ex] &= \frac{7 - (4, 8, 9)}{12} \\[2ex] &= \frac{(+3, -1, -2)}{12}. \end{aligned}
減四分之三者二，減三分之二者一，并以益三分之一，各平於一十二分之七。
That diminished of the three of four shares (3/4), is two (2); that diminished of the two of three shares (2/3), one (1); combined to benefit the one of three shares (1/3), each level at seven of twelve (7/12) shares.

Vol. II ¶5. Volume conversions for grain

[▲ Paragraphs] [manuscript] A30 B153(148) C52142(2.5) D42
今有粟一斗。問為糲米幾何？
答曰：六升。
Now there be grain, one (1) 斗 (tau) (tou) (dou). We ask: how much be this in coarse grain?
Answer saith: six (6) 升 (shing) (shêng) (sheng).
  • Version C erroneously has 糯米 for 糲米.
術曰：置粟一斗，十升。以糲米率三十乘之，得三百升為實。以粟率五十為法，除之，即得。
Method saith: put down the grain, one (1) 斗 (tau) (tou) (dou), or ten (10) 升 (shing) (shêng) (sheng). Multiplying it by the coarse grain rate, thirty (30), resulteth in three hundred (300) 升 (shing) (shêng) (sheng) as the dividend. Use the grain rate, fifty (50), as the divisor. Dividing them, we are done.

In modern notation, with V for volume and r for rate:

\begin{aligned} V(\text{sought}) &= \frac{ V(\text{there be}) \cdot r(\text{sought}) }{ r(\text{there be}) } \\[2ex] &= \frac{10 \unit{升} \times 30}{50} \\[2ex] &= 6 \unit{升} \end{aligned}
  • Here we have cross-multiplication, known as 今有術, the 'now there be method', see the chapter 〈粟米〉, 'Grain', of 《九章算術》, "Nine Chapters on Computational Methods", which gives a list of 率, rates, for common grains. (Note that the ctext database's version has transcription errors; see instead the 《四部叢刊初編》 version). The portion thereof which is relevant to this paragraph (¶5) through ¶8:
    粟米之法：粟率五十；糲米三十；粺米二十七；糳米二十四；御米二十一；……
    The method of grain: the grain rate is fifty (50); coarse grain, thirty (30); fine grain, twenty-seven (27); intricate grain, twenty-four (24); poppy seed, twenty-one (21); ...
    今有術曰：以所有數乘所求率為實，以所有率為法，實如法而一。
    The 'now there be' method saith: use the quantity of that there be, times the rate of that sought, as the dividend, and use the rate of that there be, as the divisor. Take the dividend, as if the divisor were one.

Vol. II ¶6. Volume conversions for grain

[▲ Paragraphs] [manuscript] A30 B153(148) C52145(2.6) D42
今有粟二斗一升。問為粺米幾何？
答曰：一斗一升五十分升之一十七。
Now there be grain, two (2) 斗 (tau) (tou) (dou) and one (1) 升 (shing) (shêng) (sheng). We ask: how much be this in fine grain?
Answer saith: one (1) 斗 (tau) (tou) (dou), one (1) 升 (shing) (shêng) (sheng) and seventeen fiftieths (17/50) of a 升 (shing) (shêng) (sheng).
  • Version C erroneously has 稗 for 粺, both here and below.
  • 粺： fine grain
    粺， Cantonese: 《分韻撮要》 p'ei4; 《正韻》薄邁切, paai6, Mandarin: bài
  • 五十分升之一十七： literally seventeen (17) of fifty shares (/50) of a 升 (shing) (shêng) (sheng)
術曰：置粟二十一升。以粺米率二十七乘之，得五百六十七升為實。以粟率五十為法，除之。不盡，以法而命分。
Method saith: put down the grain, twenty-one (21) 升 (shing) (shêng) (sheng). Multiplying it by the fine grain rate, twenty-seven (27), resulteth in five hundred and sixty-seven (567) 升 (shing) (shêng) (sheng) as the dividend. Use the grain rate, fifty (50), as the divisor. Divide them. For the remainder, name the fraction using the divisor.
V = \frac{21 \unit{升} \times 27}{50} = 11 \tfrac{17}{50} \unit{升}

Vol. II ¶7. Volume conversions for grain

[▲ Paragraphs] [manuscript] A30 B154(149) C52148(2.7) D43
今有粟四斗五升。問為糳米幾何？
答曰：二斗一升五分升之三。
Now there be grain, four (4) 斗 (tau) (tou) (dou) and five (5) 升 (shing) (shêng) (sheng). We ask: how much be this in intricate grain?
Answer saith: two (2) 斗 (tau) (tou) (dou), one (1) 升 (shing) (shêng) (sheng) and three fifths (3/5) of a 升 (shing) (shêng) (sheng).
  • Version C erroneously has 槃 for 糳, both here and below.
術曰：置粟四十五升。以二約糳米率二十四，得一十二，乘之，得五百四十升為實。
Method saith: put down the grain, forty-five (45) 升 (shing) (shêng) (sheng). Reducing the intricate grain rate, twenty-four (24), by two (2), resulteth in twelve (12); multiplying them resulteth in five hundred and forty (540) 升 (shing) (shêng) (sheng) as the dividend.
  • 以二約： reducing by two
    The intricate grain rate 24 here and the (normal) grain rate 50 below are reduced by their greatest common divisor, \gcd (24, 50) = 2.
以二約粟率五十，得二十五為法。除之。不盡，以等數約之，而命分。
Reducing the grain rate, fifty (50), by two (2), resulteth in twenty-five (25) as the divisor. Divide them. For the remainder, reduce it by the equal quantity, and name the fraction.
  • 不盡，以等數約之： for the remainder, reduce it by the equal quantity
    The division 540 ÷ 25 leaves remainder 15/25. Their "equal quantity" (see ¶1) is \gcd (15, 25) = 5, by which the fraction is reduced to 3/5.
V = \frac{45 \unit{升} \times 24 \div 2}{50 \div 2} = 21 \tfrac{3}{5} \unit{升}

Vol. II ¶9. Area of a rectangle

[▲ Paragraphs] [manuscript] A32 B154(149) C52154(2.9) D44
今有屋基，南北三丈，東西六丈，欲以甎砌之。凡積二尺，用甎五枚。問計幾何？
Now there be an house foundation, three (3) 丈 (chœng) (chang) (zhang) north-to-south, and six (6) 丈 (chœng) (chang) (zhang) east-to-west, and we wish to build it with bricks. Every two (2) 尺 (ch'ek) (ch'ih) (chi) of area useth five (5) bricks. We ask: how many amounteth this to?
  • 南北： north-to-south; literally south-to-north
  • Version C erroneously has ● (U+25CF) for both instances of 甎.
  • 枚
    Classifier for bricks, omitted in the English.
答曰：四千五百枚。
Answer saith: four thousand five hundred (4500) bricks.
術曰：置東西六丈，以南北三丈乘之，得一千八百尺。以五乘之，得九千尺。以二除之，即得。
Method saith: put down the six (6) 丈 (chœng) (chang) (zhang) east-to-west. Multiplying it by the three (3) 丈 (chœng) (chang) (zhang) north-to-south, resulteth in one thousand eight hundred (1800) 尺 (ch'ek) (ch'ih) (chi).     The "斛 (huk) (hu) (hu) divisor" gives the conversion between the volume unit cubic 尺 (ch'ek) (ch'ih) (chi) and the capacity unit 斛 (huk) (hu) (hu). In modern (dimensionally consistent) notation, we would write
    1 = \frac{1.62 \unit{尺}^3}{\unit{斛}}.
    Recall the Chinese did not have a fully developed notion of dimensional analysis, so square 尺 (ch'ek) (ch'ih) (chi) and cubic 尺 (ch'ek) (ch'ih) (chi) were simply referred to as 尺 (ch'ek) (ch'ih) (chi) (though they still knew how to do area and volume conversions correctly). But
    \begin{aligned} 1.62 \unit{尺}^3 &= (1 \unit{尺} + 6 \unit{寸} + 2 \unit{分}) \cdot \unit{尺}^2 \\ &= 1 \unit{尺}^3 + 600 \unit{寸}^3 + 20000 \unit{分}^3 \\ &\ne 1 \unit{尺}^3 + 6 \unit{寸}^3 + 2 \unit{分}^3, \end{aligned}
    so parsing 尺 (ch'ek) (ch'ih) (chi), 寸 (ts'ün) (ts'un) (cun) and 分 (fan) (fên) (fen) individually as cubic units doesn't make sense.

Here \pi \approx 3 (so that 4\pi \approx 12), see Vol. I ¶5.

今有溝，廣十丈，深五丈，長二十丈，欲以千尺作一方。問得幾何？
Now there be a ditch, of width ten (10) 丈 (chœng) (chang) (zhang), depth five (5) 丈 (chœng) (chang) (zhang), and length twenty (20) 丈 (chœng) (chang) (zhang), and we wish to consider a thousand (1000) 尺 (ch'ek) (ch'ih) (chi) as one block. We ask: how many result?
答曰：一千方。
Answer saith: one thousand (1000) blocks.
術曰：置廣一十丈。以深五丈乘之，得五千尺。又以長二十丈乘之，得一百萬尺。
Method saith: put down the width, ten (10) 丈 (chœng) (chang) (zhang). Multiplying it by the depth, five (5) 丈 (chœng) (chang) (zhang), resulteth in five thousand (5000) 尺 (ch'ek) (ch'ih) (chi). Also multiplying it by the length, twenty (20) 丈 (chœng) (chang) (zhang), resulteth in one million (1000000) 尺 (ch'ek) (ch'ih) (chi).
以一千除之，即得。
Dividing it by one thousand, we are done.

In modern notation, the volume of a rectangular prism of width W = 10 \unit{丈}, depth H = 5 \unit{丈} and length L = 20 \unit{丈} is

\begin{aligned} V &= W H L \\ &= 100 \unit{尺} \times 50 \unit{尺} \times 200 \unit{尺} \div \frac{1000 \unit{尺}^3}{\unit{block}} \\[2ex] &= 1000 \unit{blocks}. \end{aligned}

Vol. II ¶19. Rod calculus: square root algorithm

[▲ Paragraphs] [manuscript] A40 B156(151) C52186(2.19) D52
今有積二十三萬四千五百六十七步。問為方幾何？
Now there be area, two hundred and thirty-four thousand, five hundred and sixty-seven (234567) 步 (pou) (pu) (bu). We ask: be this a square of edge how much?
答曰：四百八十四步九百六十八分步之三百一十一。
Answer saith: four hundred and eighty-four (484) 步 (pou) (pu) (bu) and three hundred and eleven nine hundred and sixty-eighths (311/968) of a 步 (pou) (pu) (bu).

Here we have an algorithm for computing the square root, in this instance giving

\sqrt{234567 \unit{步}^2} \approx 484 \tfrac{311}{968} \unit{步}.
術曰：置積二十三萬四千五百六十七步為實。
Method saith: put down the area, two hundred and thirty-four thousand, five hundred and sixty-seven (234567) 步 (pou) (pu) (bu), as the dividend.

The radicand is called the dividend (實),

d := 234567.
次借一算為下法。步之，超一位，至百而止。
Next, borrow one rod, to be the lower divisor. Step it forth, jumping over one place, even unto the hundreds (100s), and stop.
  • 超：跳也； jumping over; or skipping
    Taking double steps, so that 100 is in fact 10000.

Since the number of digits of the radicand is

N := \lfloor \log_{10} d \rfloor + 1 = 6,

the number of digits of the integer part of its square root is

n := \left\lceil \frac{N}{2} \right\rceil = 3.

We then take n - 1 = 2 double steps for the lower divisor (下法)

L := (10 ^ {n - 1}) ^ 2 = 10^4.
上商置四百於實之上。
For the upper quotient, put down four hundred (400) above the dividend.
  • Versions A and C have just 商 for 上商.
  • 上商： upper quotient
    The integer part of the square root to be computed.

The hundreds digit of the upper quotient (上商) is the largest a such that a^2 L \le d, or

10000 a^2 \le 234567,

i.e.

a := 4.
副置四萬於實之下，下法之上，名為方法。
Subsidiarily, put down forty thousand (40000) below the dividend, and above the lower divisor; its name be the upright divisor.
  • 方： (morally) upright
    方法 is one of three named divisors whose names relate to integrity:
    • 方法： upright divisor
    • 廉法： incorrupt divisor
    • 隅法： honest divisor
    These shall henceforth be referred to as the straight divisors (straight as in 'straight copper' vs 'bent copper').

The first straight divisor is the upright divisor (方法)

p := a L = 4 \times 10000 = 40000.
命上商四百除實。
Command the upper quotient's four hundred (400), and remove this from the dividend.
  • 除： remove this from
    This is also the word for division. Division was thought of as repeatedly removing the divisor from the dividend.

As written, the text says d := d - 400^2, but for the purpose of understanding how this step fits in with the others, this is better written

\begin{aligned} d &:= d - a p \\ &= 234567 - 4 \times 40000 \\ &= 74567. \end{aligned}
除訖，倍方法。
The removal finished, double the upright divisor.
p := 2 p = 2 \times 40000 = 80000
方法一退，下法再退。
The upright divisor retreateth once; the lower divisor retreateth again.
  • Versions A and C are missing 方法 in 方法一退.
  • 再： again; or twice
\begin{aligned} p &:= p / 10 = 80000 / 10 = 8000 \\ L &:= L / 10^2 = 10000 / 100 = 100 \end{aligned}
復置上商八十，以次前商。
Put again for the upper quotient eighty (80), to be next after the quotient digit before.

The tens digit of the upper quotient (上商) is the largest b such that b (p + b L) \le d, or

b (8000 + 100 b) \le 74567,

i.e.

b := 8.
副置八百於方法之下，下法之上，名為廉法。
Subsidiarily, put down eight hundred (800) below the upright divisor, and above the lower divisor; its name be the incorrupt divisor.
  • 廉： incorrupt

The second straight divisor is the incorrupt divisor (廉法)

q := b L = 8 \times 100 = 800.
