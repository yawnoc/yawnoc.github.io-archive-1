《太平山獅子亭記》 or
"Record of the Lion's Pavilion, Mount 太平 (T'aai P'ing) (T'ai P'ing) (Tai Ping)"

First created: 20190407
Last modified: 20191031

Introduction

This is the text on a plaque in the Lion's Pavilion, Victoria Peak, Hong Kong, which I came across during a visit in December 2017. I only took one picture of the plaque and the angle isn't perfect, but the text is clearly legible in the full-size version, despite it having been compressed.

Unfortunately I neglected to take a picture showing the rest of the plaque. From memory it contained a long list of names, probably of people who had chipped in to fund it. Further viewing:

  • Wikimedia Commons: Category:Victoria Peak
  • Wikimedia Commons: Lions Pavilion (Victoria Peak)

Translation

Romanisation (F2): 音標 (yam peeu) (yin piao) (yin biao)

This is one of the few texts where the inserted punctuation is my own. Arguably the text is not fully literary; many bits do read like vernacular.

《太平山獅子亭記》
"Record of the Lion's Pavilion, Mount 太平 (T'aai P'ing) (T'ai P'ing) (Tai Ping)"
  • 太平山： Mount 太平 (T'aai P'ing) (T'ai P'ing) (Tai Ping)； literally Mount Peace; or Mount Tranquility
    Victoria Peak, or simply The Peak.
太平山，古稱鼇峰，南臨大海，北與九龍諸山遥遥相望，岡巒連綿，林木蔚茂，風景絶美。
Mount 太平 (T'aai P'ing) (T'ai P'ing) (Tai Ping), in ancient times referred to as 鼇 (Ngou) (Ao) (Ao) Peak, overlooks the great sea unto the south, and is in far, far mutual looking unto the north with the multiple mountains of 九龍 (Kau Lung) (Chiu Lung) (Jiu Long); its ridges and small, pointed hills are connected and unbroken, its woods and trees thriving and flourishing, and its sights and scenery absolutely beautiful.
  • 鼇：海中大鱉也； a great turtle of the sea (according to legend)
    鼇，牛刀切， Cantonese: ngou4, Mandarin áo
  • 峰： Peak; or Summit
  • 臨：居高臨下；臨，視也； overlooks; or looks down from above
  • 九龍： Nine Dragons
    Kowloon.
  • 岡：山脊也； ridges (as in mountain ridges); literally mountain backs
  • 巒：山小而銳；又山綿連曰巒； small, pointed hills; also unbroken and connected hills
    巒，落元切，音鑾， Cantonese: lün4, Mandarin luán
  • 蔚：茂也； thriving; or flourishing
    蔚，威畏切，音尉， Cantonese: wai3, Mandarin wèi
我獅子會，建亭於芬尼徑側，為主亭一，前亭五，如北辰居中，而五星拱之。
We the Lions Club, built the Pavilion to the side of Findlay Path, composed of one main Pavilion and five fore Pavilions, like the North Star abiding in the centre with the Five Planets surrounding it.
  • 建： built; or constructed
  • 主： main; or primary
  • 北辰： North Star
  • 居： abiding in; or dwelling in
  • 五星： the Five Planets
    These are 金木水火土, i.e. Venus, Jupiter, Mercury, Mars and Saturn respectively.
  • 拱，拱翊，環衛也： surrounding (in support or defence)
    The line 如北辰居中，而五星拱之 is a nod to Confucius in 《論語・為政》:
    子曰：「為政以德，譬如北辰，居其所而眾星共之。」
    The Master said, "He who governeth using virtue is like the North Star, abiding in its place with the multitude of stars surrounding in defence of it."
    Here, 共 is interchangeable with 拱.
丹楹畫檻，通以月門，悉依古制。
With red-painted columns and drawing-decorated balustrades, through to the Moon Gate, all were made according to the old ways.
  • 楹：柱也； columns
    楹，以成切， Cantonese: ying4, Mandarin yíng
    The phrase 丹楹 refers generally to good looking architecture, as in 丹楹刻桷, i.e. "red-painted columns and carved square rafters".
  • 畫： drawing-decorated
    畫，去聲， Cantonese: waa6, Mandarin huà
    Initially I was unsure of the reading of 畫 in Cantonese. In literary contexts, the character 畫 has two pronunciations, 入聲 (waak9) for "draw", "divide", "plan", etc., and 去聲 (waa6) for "drawing", "painting", etc. Yet when explicitly a noun, the latter of these is vernacularised into 上聲 (waa2). Now in the case of 畫檻, 畫 means "decorated with drawings", so I believe it should be read in 去聲 (waa6). My line of thinking is confirmed by at least three singers' rendering of the similar phrase 畫欄 as waa6 laan4, in the line 畫欄風擺竹橫斜 of 《再世紅梅記・脫穽救裴》:
    1. 任劍輝
    2. 龍劍笙
    3. 蓋鳴暉
    As to why the author of the plaque text chose 畫檻 (waa6 laam6) over the more common 畫欄 (waa6 laan4) whose meaning is the same: this is probably to balance the tone pattern. 丹楹畫檻 has the pattern 平平仄仄, whereas 丹楹畫欄 has 平平仄平 (which is not as nice).
  • 檻： balustrades; or rails
    檻，音艦， Cantonese: laam6, Mandarin jiàn
  • 月門： Moon Gate; or Moon Entrance
    This is the circular opening found in much traditional Chinese architecture.
  • 古： old; or ancient
春秋佳日，中西人士聨翩蒞止，登斯亭也。
On a good spring or autumn day, both Chinese and Western people continuously flock here to visit, ascending up to this Pavilion.
  • 聨翩：接連不斷；翩，疾飛也； continuously flock; literally rapidly fly together
    翩，音篇， Cantonese: p'een1, Mandarin piān
    "Flock" preserves the connotation with bird flight.
  • 蒞止：蒞，臨也； visit
    蒞，力地切，音利， Cantonese: lei6, Mandarin lì
可俯瞰維多利亞海港南北两岸，其間朝暉夕陰，晴光雨色，千變萬化，殆如畫圖。
One can face downwards and overlook the two shores, southern and northern, of Victoria Harbour. Amidst these, the morning sunshine and the even darkness, and the clear light and the rainy hues, undergo a thousand changes and a myriad tranformations, as in paintings and drawings.
  • 瞰：視也；又俯視曰瞰； overlook; or look
    瞰，音磡， Cantonese: ham3, Mandarin kàn
  • 晴： clear (as in weather); or fine
  • 殆
    Not really sure. Looking through the meanings listed in 《康熙字典》:
    1. 危也； perilous; or endangered
      Nah.
    2. 近也； close
      Maybe?
    3. 始也； begin to
      Nah.
    4. 將也，幾也； almost; or nearly
      Maybe?
    5. 志操精果謂之誠，反誠爲殆； opposite of 誠, which is 志操精果
      Nah.
    6. 與怠通 (interchangeable with 怠)
      Nah.
    7. 叶養里切，音以。《楚辭・天問》女歧縫裳，而館同爰止。何顚易厥首，而親以逢殆。
      Nah.
    《分韻撮要》 simply gives 危也；又語詞, i.e. "perilous; also a speech-assist word". I think this is the correct meaning; 請多多指教。
山中復有竒花異草可以觀賞，幽泉怪石可以遊遨。
Amidst the mountain, there are also rare flowers and strange grasses which one can see and appreciate, and secluded springs and oddly-shaped rocks which one can tour and roam.
  • 草： grasses
    More generally 草 refers to any green plant which is not a tree.
  • 賞： appreciate; or admire
  • 怪石： oddly-shaped rocks; literally strange rocks
    I note that 怪石 may also mean "rocks like jade": 怪石，似玉也, from 郭璞 (Kwok P'ok) (Kuo P'u) (Guo Pu)'s annotations of 《山海經》.
  • 遨： roam
    遨，牛到切，又牛刀切， Cantonese: ngou6; also ngou4, Mandarin áo
    《分韻撮要》 gives 陽去 (ngou6, my preferred reading), whereas everything referenced by 《康熙字典》 gives 陽平 (ngou4).
而遠近松風，又如笙竽合奏，耳目所接，無一而不足快。亦遊息之佳所也。
And the far and near wind through the pine tree is also like the 笙 (sang) (shêng) (sheng) and the 竽 (yü) (yü) (yu) playing in ensemble; among that which the ear and the eye receiveth, not one thing is not sufficient or pleasing. It is also an excellent place for travel and rest.
  • 笙 (sang) (shêng) (sheng)
    A mouth-blown free reed instrument.
  • 竽 (yü) (yü) (yu)
    Also a free reed wind instrument.
亭之建，倡自一九七三年。
The construction of the Pavilion was suggested in the year 1973.
  • 倡： suggested; or first suggested; or first advocated for
洪磋景、福伯齡，先後任會長，與諸君子規畫其事，蒙當局撥地興工。
洪磋景 (Hung Ch'or-king) (Hung Ts'o-ching) (Hong Cuojing) and 福伯齡 (Fuk Paak-ling) (Fu Po-ling) (Fu Boling) served as Club Chairman, one after the other, and planned those matters with the multiple gentlemen, and received from the local authority an allocation of land with which to begin work.
  • 先後： one after the other; literally before and after
  • 蒙： received from
    This is very formal. It almost reads like "gratefully received from".
而形制則前會長百强則師，任之不受酬。其建築布置諸費，凡共五十萬元。
And of designing the form and structure, the former club chairman 百强 (Paak-k'œng) (Pai-ch'iang) (Baiqiang) the architect served this role without receiving payment. Its various construction and arrangement costs, altogether totalled five hundred thousand dollars.
  • 百强 (Paak-k'œng) (Pai-ch'iang) (Baiqiang)
    The name of the architect. According to a separate plaque his full name is 陳百强 (Ch'an Paak-k'œng) (Ch'ên Pai-ch'iang) (Chen Baiqiang). I have no idea why he is referred to by first name only. Note that he shares the same full name with the singer Danny Chan (1958–1993).
  • 則師： architect
    The use of 則 to mean architectural drawing, as in 圖則, appears to be local to Hong Kong. And in this context it is pronounced chik7, not chak7, although I have yet to come across a dictionary which lists this pronunciation.
以一九七六年九月落成，是月三日，由市政局沙利士主席行接收禮。
It was completed in September of the year 1976, and on the 3rd day of this month, the ceremony of receiving was performed by Chairman 沙利士 (Saa-lei-see) (Sha-li-shih) (Shalishi) of the City Administration Authority.
  • 市政局： literally City Administration Authority
    Urban Council. Disbanded post 1997, in favour of new bodies appointed by the government, rather than elected by citizens.
  • 沙利士 (Saa-lei-see) (Sha-li-shih) (Shalishi)
    Arnaldo de Oliveira Sales.
吾聞獅子得金精之剛，聲吼如雷，天地皆動。
I hear that the lion receiveth the strength of the essence of the west; its sounds and roars are like thunder: all Heaven and Earth are moved by it.
  • 金精： the essence of the west
    Metal (金) is the element associated with the cardinal direction west.
  • 剛： strength; or toughness
今兹之舉，賴諸君子如響斯應，相輔相成，其力不啻雄獅。
This deed, here and now, dependeth upon the multiple gentlemen, having sounded a response to the calls, mutually assisting and mutually fulfilling, their efforts no less than that of the mighty lion.
  • 今兹： here and now; literally now and here
  • 舉： deed; or commendable action
  • 力： efforts; literally strength
  • 啻： less than; literally only; or merely
    啻，音翅， Cantonese: ch'ee3, Mandarin chì
尤望來遊者，亦步健神旺，顧盼自如，而與獅子同也。
I especially hope for the traveller who cometh, to also have an healthy step and an energetic spirit, and look back and look forth according to his own will, and be the same as a lion.
  • 望： hope; or wish
  • 神： spirit; or mind
爰書，是以記之。
A prisoner's document: this to record these things.
  • 爰書： a prisoner's document; or my writing is but a prisoner's document
    爰，云官切，又羽元切， Cantonese: woon4; also yün4, Mandarin yuán
    《分韻撮要》 gives woon4 (my preferred reading), whereas 《唐韻》 as referenced by 《康熙字典》 gives yün4. 爰書 is an affidavit given by a prisoner, the term supposedly derived from the text of the affidavit being changed: 爰，易也，換也。 Here the author of the plaque text closes by self-deprecating his own work.
