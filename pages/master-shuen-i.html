  • 稱： measuring of weight; or weighing
    稱， Cantonese: 去聲 ch'ing3, Mandarin 平聲 chēng
    Here 稱 is the verb "weigh", and Cantonese and Mandarin disagree on whether it should be read in 去聲 (ch'ing3/chèng) or 平聲 (ch'ing1/chēng). In Cantonese, 稱 is read in 去聲 (ch'ing3) both when it is the verb "weigh" or the noun "scales". In Mandarin, 稱 is read in 平聲 (chēng) for the verb "weigh", but 去聲 (chèng) for the noun "scales". 《康熙字典》 does not resolve this:
    • Under 處陵切 (i.e. 平聲 ch'ing1/chēng), it gives
      1. 知輕重也, "to know lightness and heaviness", corresponding to the verb "weigh".
    • Under 昌證切 (i.e. 去聲 ch'ing3/chèng) it gives both
      1. 權衡正斤兩者, "weighing scales, that for obtaining the correct 斤 and 兩", corresponding to the noun "scales"; and
      2. 又度也，量也, "also measuring; gauging", corresponding to the verb "weigh". This comes with an example from 《易・謙卦》: 君子以稱物平施, "the gentleman, by weighing up things, gives (or applies) them evenly".
