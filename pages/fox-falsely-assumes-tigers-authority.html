《狐假虎威》 or
"The Fox Falsely Assumes the Tiger's Authority"

First created: 20190407
Last modified: 20191221

Introduction

The idiom 狐假虎威 is used to describe the act of falsely assuming the authority of someone else who is higher up or more powerful, in order to oppress, intimidate or bully other people, usually the weak and vulnerable. It originates from a fable told in 《戰國策・楚策一》, or "楚 (Ch'or) (Ch'u) (Chu) Strategies I" of "The Warring States' Strategies", compiled/edited by 劉向 (Lau Hœng) (Liu Hsiang) (Liu Xiang).

Translation

Romanisation (F2): 音標 (yam peeu) (yin piao) (yin biao)

Chinese text taken from Chinese Text Project: 先秦兩漢・史書・戰國策・楚策・楚一・荊宣王問群臣.

《狐假虎威》
"The Fox Falsely Assumes the Tiger's Authority"
  • 狐
    The fox is as cunning an animal in the East, as he is in the West.
  • 假： falsely assumes (as in takes on, or adopts); or falsely borrows (without permission)
  • 威： authority; literally might
荊宣王問群臣曰：「吾聞北方之畏昭奚恤也，果誠何如？」群臣莫對。
King 宣 (Sün) (Hsüan) (Xuan) of 荊 (King) (Ching) (Jing) asked his group of ministers, saying, "I hear of the North's fear of 昭奚恤 (Ch'eeu Hai-s_t) (Chao Hsi-hsü) (Zhao Xixu); if it be true, what of it?" And his group of ministers replied not.
  • 荊 (King) (Ching) (Jing)
    Another name for the state of 楚 (Ch'or) (Ch'u) (Chu).
  • 群： group; or gathering
  • 昭奚恤 (Ch'eeu Hai-s_t) (Chao Hsi-hsü) (Zhao Xixu)
    The name of a high military official of 楚 (Ch'or) (Ch'u) (Chu).
江一對曰：「虎求百獸而食之，得狐。
But 江一 (Kong Yat) (Chiang I) (Jiang Yi) replied, saying, "The tiger sought the many beasts to eat them, and obtained the fox.
  • 江一 (Kong Yat) (Chiang I) (Jiang Yi)
    A minister of King 宣 (Sün) (Hsüan) (Xuan) of 荊 (King) (Ching) (Jing). In another manuscript he is called 江乙 (Kong Yüt) (Chiang I) (Jiang Yi).
  • 求：索也； sought
  • 百獸： the many beasts; literally the hundred beasts
  • 得： obtained; or got
狐曰：『子無敢食我也。天帝使我長百獸，今子食我，是逆天帝命也。
"And the fox said, 'Thou darest not eat me. For the Emperor of Heaven made me leader of the many beasts; shouldst thou now eat me: this would defy the command of the Emperor of Heaven.
  • 天帝： the Emperor of Heaven; or God
  • 長：掌管，領導； leader
    長，上聲， Cantonese: chœng2, Mandarin: zhǎng
  • 逆： defy; or go against
  • 命： command; or order; or mandate
子以我為不信，吾為子先行，子隨我後，觀百獸之見我而敢不走乎？』
" 'And shouldst thou be unbelieving of me, let me walk before thee and let thee follow behind me, and let us see who of the many beasts shall see me and dare run not?'
  • 先： before; or in front of
  • 後： behind; or after
虎以為然，故遂與之行。獸見之皆走。
"The tiger thought thus, therefore he then walked with him. And the beasts, seeing him, all ran.
  • 然： thus; or so
    Colloquially, 虎以為然 might be rendered as "the tiger thought this for real".
虎不知獸畏己而走也，以為畏狐也。
"The tiger knew not that the beasts feared himself and ran, but thought that they feared the fox.
今王之地方五千里，帶甲百萬，而專屬之昭奚恤；
"Today King's territory spanneth a circumference of five thousand 里 (lei) (li) (li), with armour-donning soldiers a million, and exclusively subordinate are they unto 昭奚恤 (Ch'eeu Hai-s_t) (Chao Hsi-hsü) (Zhao Xixu);
  • 地： territory; literally land
  • 方： circumference
  • 里 (lei) (li) (li)
    A unit of distance, of the order half a kilometre.
  • 百萬： a million; or in their millions; literally an hundred myriad
  • 屬： subordinate to; or belonging to
故北方之畏奚恤也，其實畏王之甲兵也，猶百獸之畏虎也。」
"Therefore, as to the North's fear of 奚恤 (Hai-s_t) (Hsi-hsü) (Xixu): it is actually fear of King's armoured troops, like the many beasts' fear of the tiger."
