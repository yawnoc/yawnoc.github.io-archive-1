《孔子問答：小兒論》 or
"Q&A with Confucius: the Little Child's Discourse"

First created: 20190407
Last modified: 20191109

Introduction

This is one of several accounts of 項橐 (Hong T'ok) (Hsiang T'o) (Xiang Tuo), a child prodigy who is supposed to have outwitted Confucius at the age of seven. From 《淮南子・脩務訓》： 「夫項託七歲為孔子師」, meaning "項橐 (Hong T'ok) (Hsiang T'o) (Xiang Tuo) at seven years became teacher of Master 孔 (Hung) (K'ung) (Kong)". In 《三字經》 he is mentioned in the line 「昔仲尼，師項橐」 meaning "仲尼 (Chung-nei) (Chung-ni) (Zhongni) of old, had 項橐 (Hong T'ok) (Hsiang T'o) (Xiang Tuo) for his teacher".

The text is sourced from a 1999 (己卯年) Chinese almanac (通勝), printed by 天寶樓 (though these days only two almanac printing houses remain in Hong Kong, 永經堂 and 廣經堂). It is entitled 小兒論, with the heading 孔子問答 in the margins. It is relatively easy to read (and arguably isn't fully literary), and I would recommend it as a beginning text.

Although there is no paragraphing, 句讀 (k_ü tau) (chü tou) (ju dou) are supplied (albeit with errors in a few places). These are the rudimentary punctuation markers

  • 句（。） for the end of a sentence
  • 讀（、） for a mid-sentence pause
    讀，去聲， Cantonese: tau6, Mandarin dòu

although the almanac uses 句 even for mid-sentence pauses, and only uses 讀 (very sparingly) for lists; see the actual first page of the text in the almanac.

Translation

Romanisation (F2): 音標 (yam peeu) (yin piao) (yin biao)
《孔子問答：小兒論》
"Q&A with Master 孔 (Hung) (K'ung) (Kong): the Little Child's Discourse"
  • 孔子： Master 孔 (Hung) (K'ung) (Kong)
    Confucius.
  • 問答： Q&A; or questions and answers; or asking and replying
  • 小： little; or small
  • 論： discourse; also theory
孔子，名丘，字仲尼，設敎於魯國之西。
Master 孔 (Hung) (K'ung) (Kong), named 丘 (Yau) (Ch'iu) (Qiu) and styled 仲尼 (Chung-nei) (Chung-ni) (Zhongni), established a teaching in the west of the nation of 魯 (Lou) (Lu) (Lu).
  • 字： styled
    名 is one's name given at birth, whereas 字 is given at twenty (adulthood) by one's parents or teacher, usually rendered as "courtesy name" or "style name" (although hardly anyone has a 字 these days). I prefer the rendering "style name" because I can use the verb "styled". In modern vernacular Mandarin, people say 名字 for name, when technically they mean 名; this is to do with Mandarin having (on average) more homophones than other variants of Chinese, hence the need to include more syllables (in this case "字") to disambiguate.
  • 敎： a teaching
    Arguably "a religion" would also work.
一日率諸弟子，御車出遊，路逢數兒嬉戲，中有一兒不戲。
One day he led his many disciples out for travel, driving his chariot. Along the road they encountered several children playing; and amidst them there was one child who played not.
  • 御：使馬也； driving
    Literally "using horses".
  • 車： chariot
    車，音居， Cantonese: k_ü1, Mandarin jū
    This is a literary text, and Confucius & co. are riding in a chariot, not a small cart or a wheelbarrow. Therefore, please do not pronounce this ch'e1/chē: my ears will bleed.
孔子乃駐車問曰：「獨汝不戲，何也？」
Master 孔 (Hung) (K'ung) (Kong) thus stopped his chariot and asked, saying, "Alone thou playest not: why?"
  • 駐：馬止也； stopped
    Literally "stopping the horses".
小兒答曰：「凢戲無益，衣破難縫。上辱父母，下及門中。必有鬥爭，勞而無功。豈爲好事？故乃不戲。」
The little child replied, saying, "All play is of no benefit: clothes damaged are hard to mend; an insult to father and mother above: even down unto those amidst the family; surely there shall be fighting and disputes: toil, but no achievement. How be this a good thing? Therefore I play not."
  • 凢
    Variant of 凡.
  • 縫： mend; literally stitch
  • 門中：門，家門； those amidst the family
    His siblings.
  • 功： achievement; or (good) end result
  • 豈： how
    A very stern and righteous "how".
遂低頭以瓦片作城。
He then lowered his head and began building a wall using some tile pieces.
  • 低頭： lowered his head
    Being a little child, he had to look up to speak to Confucius; having finished speaking he turned his head back down. From the illustration in the almanac, he then joins in with the building of the wall:
  • 片： pieces; literally slices
孔子責之曰：「何不避車乎？」
And Master 孔 (Hung) (K'ung) (Kong) rebuked him, saying, "Wherefore avoidest thou not the path of my chariot?"
小兒答曰：「自古及今，爲當車避于城，不當城避於車。」
The little child replied, saying, "Since antiquity, even until now, be it that the chariot ought to avoid the wall, not that the wall ought to avoid the chariot."
  • 當： ought to; or should
孔子乃勒車論道，下車而問焉：「汝年尚幼，何多詐乎？」
Master 孔 (Hung) (K'ung) (Kong) thus restrained his chariot to reason with him, alighting his chariot and asking, "Thou art still young in years; wherefore so much cunning?"
  • 論道： reason with; literally debate logic with
    Similar to 講道理, meaning "talk some sense with". My initial interpretation of 論道 when reading the text was "argue for the right of way"; while probably not the intended meaning, I like it so much I've included it here.
  • 詐： cunning; literally trickery; or deception
    Here I think Confucius is referring to the child's wit; I don't see how the child is tricking or deceiving Confucius in any way.
小兒答曰：「人生三歲，分別父母。兔生三日，走地畎畝。魚生三日，遊於江湖。天生自然，豈謂詐乎？」
The little child replied, saying, "I the human lived three years, and was separated from my father and mother; the rabbit liveth three days, and runneth upon the ditches and fields of the earth; the fish liveth three days, and journeyeth unto the rivers and lakes. Naturally are they automatically thus: how be this cunning?"
  • 人生： I the human lived
    Literally this is "human life", or "life". To match the pattern in the sentences which follow, 人生三歲 might be rendered "man liveth three years".
  • 三歲： three years; or to the age of three
  • 畎：田中溝也； ditch (in a field)
    畎，音犬， Cantonese: hün2, Mandarin quǎn
  • 天生： naturally; literally born of nature; or born of Heaven
  • 豈謂： how be this; literally how speak of this as
孔子曰：「汝居何鄉、何里、何姓、何名、何字？」
Master 孔 (Hung) (K'ung) (Kong) said, "Dwellest thou in what hometown and what village? What surname, what name and what style name hast thou?"
小兒答曰：「吾居敝鄉、賤地、姓項、名橐、未有字也。」
The little child replied, saying, "I dwell in a shabby hometown and a lowly village, and am surnamed 項 (Hong) (Hsiang) (Xiang) and named 橐 (T'ok) (T'o) (Tuo), and am yet to have a style name."
  • 敝： shabby; or worn-out
    Both 敝 and 賤 can be used as a self-deprecating substitute for "I" or "my".
孔子曰：「吾欲共汝同遊，汝意下如何？」
Master 孔 (Hung) (K'ung) (Kong) said, "I wish to travel together with thee: what be thy thoughts upon this?"
  • Beware stranger danger.
  • 汝意下如何： what be thy thoughts upon this; or what sayest thou
小兒答曰：「家有嚴父，須當事之。家有慈母，須當養之。
The little child replied, saying, "In mine house there is my strict father: I must attend to him. In mine house there is my loving mother: I must provide for her.
  • 慈： loving; or kind
    The ideal father is strict (嚴) in that in addition to providing for his children, he ought not to fail in teaching them (as in 養不教，父之過 meaning "to provide for but not teach, is the mistake of the father"). The ideal mother is loving (慈). In literary Chinese the words 嚴 and 慈 can, even on their own, mean father and mother respectively.
  • 養： provide for; or support; or feed
家有賢兄，須當順之。家有弱弟，須當敎之。家有明師，須當學之。何暇同遊也？」
"In mine house there are my worthy older brothers: I must obey them. In mine house there are my feeble younger brothers: I must teach them. In mine house there is my brilliant teacher: I must learn from him. What spare time have I to travel together with thee?"
  • 賢： worthy; also virtuous
    Often used as an honorific.
  • 順： obey; or go along with; or follow
  • 明：高明； brilliant; or wise
  • 暇：閒也； spare time; or free time
孔子曰：「吾車中有三十二棋子，與汝奕博，汝意下如何？」
Master 孔 (Hung) (K'ung) (Kong) said, "In my chariot there are thirty-two chess pieces, to play chess for a wager with thee: what be thy thoughts upon this?"
  • 三十二棋子
    It is a coincidence that Chinese Chess (象棋) and Western Chess both consist of 16 chess pieces per player. Of course this text is not canon, nor is it historically accurate; in Confucius's time, 奕 referred to the Surrounding Game (圍棋) (also known as Go).
小兒答曰：「天子好博，四海不理。諸侯好博，有妨政紀。士儒好博，學問廢弛。
The little child replied, saying, "Be the Son of Heaven fond of wagering: all within the Four Seas shall not be properly administered. Be the many lords fond of wagering: this doth harm governance and law. Be the scholars and academics fond of wagering: scholarship shall be neglected and abandoned.
  • 天子： the Son of Heaven
    The emperor.
  • 理： administered; or managed
  • 侯： lords; or marquesses
    See 諸侯.
  • 妨： harm; also obstruct
  • 士儒： scholars and academics
    Both 士 and 儒 refer to scholars, although 儒 can mean Confucian scholar specifically. I have used "academics" to avoid rendering 士儒 as "scholars and scholars".
  • 學問： scholarship; or learning; literally learning and enquiry
  • 弛：放也，棄也，廢也； abandoned; literally slackened
小人好博，輸却家計。奴婢好博，必受鞭撲。農夫好博，耕種失時。是故不博也。」
"Be the little man fond of wagering: he shall lose away the livelihood of his family. Be the manservants and maidservants fond of wagering: they shall surely be lashed and beaten. Be the farmer fond of wagering: he shall plough and sow out of season. For these reasons I wager not."
  • 小人： the little man; or the despicable man
    In Confucianism, 小人 is a person of low moral character, always seeking quick personal gain. The opposite of 小人 is 君子 (gentleman), a refined man and a person of virtue. James Legge renders 君子 as "the superior man" and 小人 as "the mean man" in 《論語・述而》.
  • 撲：擊也； beaten
  • 失時： out of season; literally missing the season
孔子曰：「吾欲與汝平却天下，汝意下如何？」
Master 孔 (Hung) (K'ung) (Kong) said, "I wish to flatten away all under Heaven with thee: what be thy thoughts upon this?"
  • 平却： flatten away; or level out
    Alternatively, 平 may mean "fair" (as in fairness, or egalitarianism).
  • 天下： all under Heaven
    The whole world. Similar to "under the sun" in English.
小兒答曰：「天下不可平也。或有高山，或有江湖。或有王侯，或有奴婢。
The little child replied, saying, "All under Heaven cannot be flattened. Perhaps there be high mountains; perhaps there be rivers and lakes; perhaps there be kings and lords; perhaps there be manservants and maidservants.
平却高山，鳥獸無依。塡却江湖，魚鱉無歸。除却王侯，民多是非。絶却奴婢，君子使誰？天下蕩蕩，豈可平乎？」
"Flatten away the high mountains, and the birds and beasts shall have nothing to lean upon; fill away the rivers and lakes, and the fishes and turtles shall have no home; remove away the kings and lords, and the people shall have many quarrels; cut off the manservants and maidservants, and whom shall the rulers employ? Vast be all under Heaven: how can it be flattened?"
  • 鱉：龜屬； turtle
    鱉，變入聲， Cantonese: peet8, Mandarin biē
  • 無歸： no home; or nothing to return to
  • 絶却： cut off; literally cut away
  • 君子： rulers
    Here I don't think 君子 refers to gentlemen, but to rulers and lords (君 as in "monarch" or "sovereign").
  • 使： employ; or use
  • 蕩蕩：大也，廣遠也； vast
孔子曰：「汝知天下，何火無烟？何水無魚？何山無石？何樹無枝？何人無婦？何女無夫？
Master 孔 (Hung) (K'ung) (Kong) said, "Knowest thou under Heaven: What fire hath no smoke? What water hath no fish? What mountain hath no rock? What tree hath no twig? What man hath no wife? What woman hath no husband?
  • 火： fire; also light
  • 山： mountain; also hill
  • 枝： twig; also branch
何牛無犢？何馬無駒？何雄無雌？何雌無雄？何爲君子？何爲小人？何爲不足？何爲有餘？何城無市？何人無字？」
"What cow hath no calf? What horse hath no foal? What male bird hath no female bird? What female bird hath no male bird? What be the gentleman? What be the little man? What be not sufficient? What be in excess? What city hath no market? What man hath no style name?"
  • 犢：牛子也； calf
    犢，音獨， Cantonese: tuk9, Mandarin dú
  • 駒：馬二歲曰駒； foal; literally horse under two years
  • 雄： male bird
    More generally this refers to any male plant or animal.
  • 雌： female bird
    More generally this refers to any female plant or animal.
小兒答曰：「螢火無烟。井水無魚。土山無石。枯樹無枝。仙人無婦。玉女無夫。
The little child replied, saying, "Firefly light hath no smoke. Well water hath no fish. The dirt hill hath no rock. The withered tree hath no twig. The celestial man hath no wife. The fairy woman hath no husband.
  • 螢火： firefly light
    This riddle only works because 火 may mean both "fire" and "light".
  • 土山： the dirt hill; or the earth hill
    Similarly this works because 山 may mean both "mountain" and "hill".
  • 仙人： the celestial man
    A supernatural, immortal being, usually celibate. Many people attempted to become immortal by ingesting various "immortality elixirs" of alchemy, which were usually toxic substances.
  • 玉女： fairy lady; literally jade lady
    The female equivalent of 仙人.
土牛無犢。木馬無駒。孤雄無雌。孤雌無雄。賢爲君子。愚爲小人。冬日不足。夏日有餘。皇城無市。小人無字。」
"A clay cow hath no calf. A wooden horse hath no foal. The lone male bird hath no female bird. The lone female bird hath no male bird. Virtuous be the gentleman. Foolish be the little man. Winter days be not sufficient. Summer days be in excess. The imperial city hath no market. I the little man have no style name."
  • 小人： I the little man
    A self-deprecating self address. Evidently 項橐 (Hong T'ok) (Hsiang T'o) (Xiang Tuo) is far from the little man of Confucianism.
孔子問曰：「汝知天地之綱紀，陰陽之終始？
Master 孔 (Hung) (K'ung) (Kong) asked, saying, "Knowest thou the principle and the law of Heaven and Earth, and the beginning and the end of Dark and Light?
  • 綱： principle; or crux
  • 陰陽： Dark and Light; or Shade and Sun; or Female and Male
    Usually rendered "yin and yang" in Western literature.
何爲左？何爲右？何爲表？何爲裏？何爲父？何爲母？何爲夫？何爲婦？
"What be left? What be right? What be on the surface? What be within? What be the father? What be the mother? What be the husband? What be the wife?
風從何來？雨從何至？雲從何出？霧從何起？天地相去幾千萬里？」
"From whence cometh the wind? From whence arriveth the rain? From whence emergeth the clouds? From whence ariseth the fog? How many thousands and myriads of 里 (lei) (li) (li) are Heaven and Earth mutually separated?"
  • 里 (lei) (li) (li)
    A unit of distance, of the order half a kilometre.
小兒答曰：「九九還歸八十一，是天地之綱紀。八九七十二，是陰陽之終始。
The little child replied, saying, "Nine nines return eighty-one: this be the principle and the law of Heaven and Earth. And eight nines seventy-two: this be the beginning and the end of Dark and Light.
  • There is a whole genre of stuff like this i.e. divination, numerology etc. E.g. in 《大戴禮記・易本命》:
    天一，地二，人三；三三而九，九九八十一；一主日，日數十，故人十月而生。
    Heaven is first, Earth second and Man third. Now three threes are nine, and nine nines eighty-one. One governeth the sun, and the sun hath number ten; therefore humans are born of a term of ten months.
    (Here "months" are 28-day months, so that ten months make forty weeks, a full term.) Being a physicist, I take a very dim view of such works (involving arbitrary associations etc.). It is a real pity too, for such material detracts from the serious mathematics of Classical Chinese literature.
天爲父。地爲母。日爲夫。月爲婦。東爲左。西爲右。外爲表。内爲裏。
"Heaven be the father. Earth be the mother. The sun be the husband. The moon be the wife. East be left. West be right. The exterior be on the surface. The interior be within.
  • 東爲左。西爲右。
    Traditionally, one faces the south; thus east is to the left, and west is to the right.
風從蒼梧。雨從郊市。雲從山出。霧從地起。天地相去，有千千萬萬餘里。東西南北，皆有寄耳。」
"The wind cometh from 蒼梧 (Ch'ong Ng) (Ts'ang Wu) (Cang Wu). The rain arriveth from the town of 郊 (Kaau) (Chiao) (Jiao). The clouds emergeth from the mountains. The fog ariseth from the ground. Heaven and Earth are mutually separated in excess of thousands upon thousands and myriads upon myriads of 里 (lei) (li) (li). East, west, south and north all have adherence unto these."
  • 蒼梧 (Ch'ong Ng) (Ts'ang Wu) (Cang Wu)：地名，舜葬處
    A place name. The legendary 舜 (S_n) (Shun) (Shun) is supposed to have been buried here.
  • 郊 (Kaau) (Chiao) (Jiao)：晉地名
    A place name in 晉 (Ch_n) (Chin) (Jin).
  • 寄： adherence unto; or dependence upon
    請多多指教, for I am unsure about this one.
孔子問曰：「汝言父母是親，夫婦是親？」
Master 孔 (Hung) (K'ung) (Kong) asked, saying, "Sayest thou that father and mother are more close unto their children, or that husband and wife are more close?"
  • 親： close; or loving
小兒答曰：「父母是親，夫婦不親。」
The little child replied, saying, "Father and mother are more close unto their children, and husband and wife are not as close."
孔子曰：「夫婦生則同衾，死則同穴，何得不親？」
Master 孔 (Hung) (K'ung) (Kong) said, "Husband and wife are in the same bed quilt in life, and the same grave in death: how can they be not as close?"
  • 衾： bed quilt; or blanket
小兒答曰：「人生無婦，如車無輪。無輪再造，必得其新。婦死更索，又得其新。
The little child replied, saying, "A man losing his wife in life is like a chariot losing its wheel. Should a wheel be lost, remake it: surely a new one shall be obtained. Should one's wife die, seek anew: also a new one shall be obtained.
  • 更： anew
    更，平聲， Cantonese: kang1, Mandarin gēng
賢家之女，必配貴夫。十間之室，須得棟樑。
"A daughter of a worthy family shall surely be matched with the worthy man. The bedchambers of ten partitions must get pillars and roof beams.
  • 貴： worthy; or honourable; literally noble
    Perhaps "venerable" as in "commanding respect" would also work, but 貴 has no connotation with age. Rendering both 賢 and 貴 as "worthy" is not desirable, but unavoidable.
  • I have rendered 十間之室，須得棟樑 literally, but really this is saying "the women of many families must get upholding men":
    • 十間： many families; literally ten partitions
      間，去聲， Cantonese: kaan3, Mandarin jiàn
    • 室： women; literally bedchambers
      In other contexts, 室 may mean wife or concubine.
    • 棟樑： upholding men; literally pillars and roof beams
      Those worthy of bearing great responsibility.
三窗六牗，不如一户之光。衆星朗朗，不如孤月獨明。父母之恩，奚可失也？」
"Yet the light of three windows and six casements is not as the light of one particular window. The multitude of stars bright, are not as the lone moon, radiant by itself. How can the affection of father and mother be lost?"
  • 牗：窗也； casement
    Variant of 牖.
    牗，與久切，音酉， Cantonese: yau5, Mandarin yǒu
    I didn't want to render 窗, 牗 and 户 all as "window", and "casement" was the only other word I could find in English. Technically casements are windows with side hinges, but in literary and poetic contexts, "casement" is a fancy way of referring to a window of any type.
  • 恩： affection; or love
孔子歎曰：「賢哉！賢哉！」
Master 孔 (Hung) (K'ung) (Kong) sighed, saying, "Worthy art thou! Worthy art thou!"
小兒問孔子曰：「適來問橐，橐一一答之。橐今欲求敎夫子一言，明以誨橐，幸請勿棄。」
The little child asked Master 孔 (Hung) (K'ung) (Kong), saying, "Master hath just come to ask 橐 (T'ok) (T'o) (Tuo) questions, and 橐 (T'ok) (T'o) (Tuo) hath answered them one by one. 橐 (T'ok) (T'o) (Tuo) wisheth now to request the advice of Master in one asking, to teach 橐 (T'ok) (T'o) (Tuo) with clarity, and hopeth and asketh that Master doth not abandon this request."
  • 橐 (T'ok) (T'o) (Tuo)
    The child 項橐 (Hong T'ok) (Hsiang T'o) (Xiang Tuo) is referring to himself in the third person, out of respect.
  • 求敎： request the advice; or earnestly request the advice
  • 言：問也； asking
  • 明： clarity; or illustration
  • 幸：冀也； hopeth
小兒曰：「鵝鴨何以能浮？鴻雁何以能鳴？松柏何以冬青？」
And the little child said, "Why are geese and ducks able to float? Why are swans and wild geese able to sound? Why are pine trees and cypress trees evergreen?"
  • 浮： float; or drift
  • 鴻雁： swans and wild geese
    Arguably 鴻雁 could also be the single bird "swan goose".
  • 冬青： evergreen; literally green in winter
孔子答曰：「鵝鴨能浮，皆因足方。鴻雁能鳴，皆因頸長。松柏冬青，皆因心堅。」
Master 孔 (Hung) (K'ung) (Kong) replied, saying, "Geese and ducks are able to float, all because of webbed feet. Swans and wild geese are able to sound, all because of long necks. Pine trees and cypress trees are evergreen, all because of strong cores."
  • 足方： webbed feet; literally square feet
  • 堅： strong; or firm
小兒答曰：「不然。魚鱉能浮，豈皆足方？蝦蟆能鳴，豈因頸長？綠竹冬青，豈因心堅？」
And the little child replied, saying, "Not so. Fishes and turtles can float: how have they all webbed feet? The toad can sound: how be this because of a long neck? The green bamboo is evergreen: how be this because of a strong core?"
  • 綠竹： the green bamboo
小兒又問曰：「天上零零有幾星？」
And the little child asked again, saying, "How many stars are there, scattered, in the heavens above?"
  • 零零： scattered
    As in 零碎.
孔子答曰：「適來問地，何必談天？」
Master 孔 (Hung) (K'ung) (Kong) replied, saying, "I have just come to ask regarding the earth; wherefore must we discuss the heavens?"
小兒曰：「地下碌碌有幾屋？」
And the little child said, "How many houses are there, ordinary, on the earth below?"
  • 碌碌： ordinary; or common; or everyday
    Note that 零 (ling4/líng) rhymes with 星 (sing1/xīng), and 碌 (luk7/lù) with 屋 (uk7/wū).
孔子曰：「且論眼前之事，何必談天説地？」
Master 孔 (Hung) (K'ung) (Kong) said, "And let us discuss matters before our very eyes; wherefore must we discuss the heavens and speak of the earth?"
小兒曰：「若論眼前之事，眉毛中有幾枝？」
And the little child said, "If we shall discuss matters before our very eyes, how many hairs are there amidst mine eyebrow?"
孔子笑而不答，顧謂諸弟子曰：「後生可畏！焉知來者之不如今也？」
Master 孔 (Hung) (K'ung) (Kong) smiled and replied not, and turned back and spake unto his many disciples, saying, "The young ought to be feared! For how know we, that those who come after us, be not as we who are now?"
  • 後生可畏！焉知來者之不如今也？
    This is part of an actual (as in canon) quote of Confucius in 《論語・子罕》:
    子曰：「後生可畏，焉知來者之不如今也？四十、五十而無聞焉，斯亦不足畏也已。」
    The Master said, "The young ought to be feared; for how know we, that those who come after us, be not as we who are now? By forty or fifty and be they unheard of, then be they not worthy of fear."
    • 後生： the young; literally the after-born
      This constrasts with 先生, literally "former-born", which classically was a polite address to an older, wiser person. This usage survives today in Cantonese, and also Japanese (sensei), where 先生 means teacher.
    • 畏： feared; or respected
      Here, I do not mean fear as in being afraid, but rather reverence and respect, as in fearing God. "Is formidable" is also a good rendering of 可畏.
    • 不如： not as; or not as good as
    The almanac erroneously has 焉能求者 for 焉知來者; this is called 執錯字粒, or "picking the wrong character block" in printing.
於是登車而去。
And so he boarded his chariot and departed.
詩曰：
休欺年少聰明子，
廣有英才智過人。
談論世間無限事，
分明古聖現其身。
Verse saith:
Do not bully the intelligent child, young in years,
Who greatly hath outstanding talent, and wisdom which surpasseth others.
He discusseth and discourseth the infinite matters in the world;
Clearly the ancient sages have manifested in his person.
  • 欺： bully; or pick on
  • 過： surpasseth; or exceedeth
  • 論： discourseth; or debateth
  • 分明： clearly
    Or "blatantly", but here without the negative connotation.
  • 古聖： the ancient sages
    The virtuous, wise men of antiquity. In modern times though, due to the influence of Christian literature, 聖 has come to mean "holy", or "sacred", or "saint".
  • 現： manifested; or appeared
