----
  In the preface, [sun-tzu]
  tells us that mathematics governs absolutely everything,
  and allows us to do even magical things
  (like "seek {the} whereabouts of gods {and} earth gods").
  Basically it reads like advertising material for
  prospective STEM undergraduates.
----

<#t>
  孫子曰：夫算者， |
  [sun-tzu] saith: Computation---
</#t>
====
  <li>算：
    Computation;
    \or Mathematics
  </li>
====

----
  From the next line until almost the end of the preface,
  the text consists of pairs of lines which parallel each other,
  character for character.
  This is a very common rhetorical device in Literary Chinese.
----

<#t>
  天地之經緯，群生之元首； |
  {the} warp {and} woof of Heaven {and} Earth,
  {and the} origin {and} beginning of {the} living masses;
</#t>
====
  <li>經緯：
    {the} warp {and} woof;
    \or {the} meridians {and} parallels \+
    <@>
      "Warp {and} woof"
      | https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/warp_and_woof
      | Wiktionary: warp and woof
    </@>
    translates well, both
    literally (longways and sideways threads)
    and figuratively (fundamental structure).
  </li>
====

<#t>
  五常之本末，陰陽之父母； |
  {the} beginning {and} end of {the} Five Everlasting Virtues,
  {and the} father {and} mother of Dark {and} Light;
</#t>
====
  <li>五常：
    {the} Five Everlasting Virtues \+
    <@>
      仁義禮智信
      | https://ctext.org/three-character-classic#n90581
      | 《三字經》
    </@>,
    i.e.~Humanity, Righteousness, Propriety (or Rites), Wisdom and Trust.
  </li>
  <li>本末：
    {the} beginning {and} end;
    \or {the} base {and} tip
  </li>
  <li>陰陽：
    Dark {and} Light;
    \or Shade {and} Sun;
    \or Female {and} Male \+
    <@>
      "Yin {and} Yang"
      | https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yin_and_yang
      | Wikipedia: Yin and yang
    </@>.
  </li>
====

<#t>
  星辰之建號，三光之表裏； |
  {the} establishment {and} designation of
  {the} stars {and} heavenly bodies,
  {and the} surface {and} internals of
  {the} Three Luminaries;
</#t>
====
  <li>三光：
    {the} Three Luminaries \+
    <@>
      日月星
      | https://ctext.org/three-character-classic#n90576
      | 《三字經》
    </@>,
    i.e.~the sun, the moon and the stars.
  </li>
  <li>
    <@>
      Version~C
      | https://ctext.org/sunzi-suan-jing
      | ctext.org database page
    </@>
    erroneously has 裹 for 裏.
  </li>
====

<#t>
  五行之準平，四時之終始； |
  {the} evenness {and} balance of {the} Five Elements,
  {and the} finishing {and} starting of {the} Four Seasons;
</#t>
====
  <li>五行：
    {the} Five Elements \+
    <@>
      金木水火土
      | https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wu_Xing
      | Wikipedia: Wu Xing
    </@>,
    i.e.~metal, tree, water, fire and earth.
    Perhaps better rendered as "{the} Five Phases".
  </li>
  <li>準：平也；又均也；
    evenness;
    \or levelness
  </li>
====

<#t>
  萬物之祖宗，六藝之綱紀。 |
  {the} ancestor {and} progenitor of {the} myriad things,
  {and the} principle {and} law of {the} Six Arts.
</#t>
====
  <li>萬物：
    everything;
    \lit {the} myriad things
  </li>
  <li>六藝：
    {the} Six Arts \+
    <@>
      禮樂射御書數
      | https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Six_Arts
      | Wikipedia: Six Arts
    </@>,
    i.e.~Propriety (or Rites), Music, Archery,
    (Chariot) Driving, Calligraphy and Mathematics.
  </li>
====

<#t>
  稽群倫之聚散，考二氣之降升； |
  {It} examineth {the} gathering {and} dispersion of {the} amassed kinds,
  {and} inspecteth {the} falling {and} rising of {the} Two Breaths;
</#t>
====
  <li>稽：考也；
    examineth
  </li>
  <li>群：
    amassed;
    \lit crowded
  </li>
  <li>倫：
    kinds;
    \or types
  </li>
  <li>二氣：
    {the} Two Breaths \+
    陰氣 and 陽氣.
    Recall 陰 and 陽 are
    "Dark {and} Light", "Shade {and} Sun", or "Female {and} Male".
    <@>
      氣
      | https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Qi
      | Wikipedia: Qi
    </@>
    is usually rendered as "life force",
    transliterated as <^e>Hei</^e>
    (<@>
      Cantonese
      | /cantonese/conway-romanisation
      | Conway's Custom Romanisation for Cantonese
    </@>), <^e>Ch'i</^e> (Wade-Giles) or <^e>Qi\</^e> (Pinyin).
  </li>
====

<#t>
  推寒暑之迭運，步遠近之殊同； |
  {it} deduceth {the} alternating {and} revolving of winter {and} summer,
  {and} measureth {the} disparity {and} sameness of far {and} near;
</#t>
====
  <li>寒暑：
    winter {and} summer;
    \lit cold {and} heat
  </li>
  <li>迭：更也；
    alternating
  </li>
  <li>步：
    measureth (in steps)
  </li>
====

<#t>
  觀天道精微之兆基，察地理從橫之長短； |
  {it} inspecteth {the} foundations of
    {the} intricacies {and} minutiae of Physics,
  {and} examineth {the} length of
    {the} meridians {and} parallels of Geography;
</#t>
====
  <li>天道：
    Physics;
    \lit {the} laws of Nature;
    \or {the} way of Heaven
  </li>
  <li>兆基：
    foundations \+
    According to 《國語辭典》 this means 開創基業,
    roughly "opening/beginning/foundation".
  </li>
  <li>地理：
    Geography;
    \lit {the} science of Earth
  </li>
  <li>從橫：
    meridians {and} parallels (of latitude) \+
    Variant of 縱橫.
    Here 從橫 is referring to "things of broad scope"
    or "the grand scheme of things", in contrast to 精微
    (intricacies {and} minutiae) in the previous line,
    which it is parallel with.
  </li>
  <li>長短：
    length;
    \lit length {and} shortness
  </li>
====

<#t>
  采神祇之所在，極成敗之符驗； \+
  窮道德之理，究性命之情。 |
  {it} seeketh {the} whereabouts of gods {and} earth gods,
  {and} examineth corroboration of success {and} failure; \+
  {it} exhausteth {the} reasoning of morality,
  {and} investigateth {the} sense of life.
</#t>
====
  <li>祇：地神；
    earth gods \+
      祇，音岐，
      <^cm> k'ei4 | qi/ </^cm>
  </li>
  <li>極：
    examineth ({unto} extremity) \+
    All of 采, 極, 窮 and 究
    mean "investigate", and in particular the last three mean
    "investigate to the utmost extent".
  </li>
  <li>符驗：
    corroboration;
    \lit corroboration {by} talisman
  </li>
  <li>道德：
    morality;
    \or ethics
  </li>
  <li>理：
    reasoning;
    \or logic
  </li>
====

<#t>
  立規矩，準方圓， \+
  謹法度，約尺丈， |
  {It} establisheth {the} rules,
  standardiseth the square {and} the circle, \+
  abideth by {the} law,
  agreeth upon {the} rule {and the} rod,
</#t>
====
  <li>規矩：
    {the} rules;
    \lit the compass {and} the set square
  </li>
  <li>[尺丈]：
    {the} rule {and the} rod \+
    Units of length, see
    <@> Vol.~I~\P1 | /sun-tzu/i#1 | Vol. I \P1 </@>.
    One [尺] or rule is of the order one foot,
    and one [丈] or rod is equal to ten rules.
  </li>
====

<#t>
  立權衡，平重輕， \+
  剖毫釐，析黍絫。 |
  establisheth the weighing scale,
  balanceth heavy {and} light \+
  disecteth mils {and} cents,
  {and} splitteth millets {and} piles.
</#t>
====
  <li>重輕：
    heavy {and} light;
    \or important {and} unimportant
  </li>
  <li>[毫釐]：
    mils {and} cents \+
    Small units of length
    (see <@> Vol.~I~\P1 | /sun-tzu/i#1 | Vol. I \P1 </@>),
    respectively a thousandth and a hundredth
    of the Chinese inch, [寸].
  </li>
  <li>[黍絫]：
    millets {and} piles \+
    Small units of weight,
    (see <@> Vol.~I~\P2 | /sun-tzu/i#2 | Vol. I \P2 </@>),
    respectively a hundredth and a tenth
    of the Chinese dram, [銖],
    which is of the order one gram.
  </li>
====

<#t>
  歷億載而不朽，施八極而無疆。 |
  {It} endureth {an} hundred million years and decayeth not,
  {and is} applied unto {the} Eight Extremities without bound.
</#t>
====
  <li>億：
    {an} hundred million;
    \or {a} square-myriad
  </li>
  <li>八極：
    {the} Eight Extremities \+
    Probably the four cardinal directions
    plus the four intercardinal directions.
    Sometimes called the "principal winds" in English.
  </li>
====

<#t>
  散之不可勝究，斂之不盈掌握。 |
  Scattering it, {it} cannot {be} wholly investigated,
  {yet} gathering it, {it is} not beyond grasp.
</#t>
====
  <li>勝：
    wholly;
    \or completely \+
      勝，平聲，
      <^cmg> shing1 | she=ng | she\ng </^cmg>
  </li>
  <li>斂：聚也；
    gathering
  </li>
  <li>盈：過曰盈；
    beyond;
    \lit in excess of
  </li>
====

<#t>
  嚮之者富有餘，背之者貧且窶； |
  Those who echo {in agreement with} it {shall be} rich in excess,
  {but} those who turn {their} backs to it {shall be} poor and destitute;
</#t>
====
  <li>窶：貧也；
    destitute \+
      窶，音巨，
      <^cm> k_u"6 | ju\ </^cm>
  </li>
====

<#t>
  心開者幼沖而即悟，意閉者皓首而難精。 |
  those who {are} open-minded, {even as} young children,
    {shall} immediately comprehend {it},
  {but} those who {are} close-minded,
    {even with a} bright white head {of hair},
    {shall find it} hard to {be} proficient {in it}.
</#t>
====
  <li>沖：童也；
    children
  </li>
  <li>意閉：
    close-minded \+
    This is the literal rendering;
    perhaps "narrow-minded" sounds more natural.
    I have translated both 心 and 意 as
    <span class="no-wrapping">"-minded"</span>.
  </li>
  <li>皓：光也；白也；
    bright white \+
      皓，音號，
      <^cm> hou6 | ha\o </^cm>
  </li>
====

----
  At this point the parallel pairs of lines stops.
----

<#t>
  夫欲學之者必務量能揆己，志在所專。 |
  Those who wish to learn it must seek to
  gauge {one's} abilities {and} measure oneself,
  {and put one's} will into that focused upon.
</#t>
====
  <li>量：
    gauge; or measure \+
      量，去聲，
      <^cm> loeng6 | lia\ng </^cm> \+
    量 is a funny word.
    When it is the verb "measure/gauge" applied to physical measurements
      (e.g.~volume of grain),
      it is read in 平聲 (<^e>loeng4</^e>/<^e>lia/ng</^e>),
    but when applied to figurative or not-so-physical quantities
      (e.g.~量力, "gauging strength/ability", or 量入, "gauging revenue"),
      it is read in 去聲 (<^e>loeng6</^e>/<^e>lia\ng</^e>).
    And when it is the noun "amount",
      also 去聲 (<^e>loeng6</^e>/<^e>lia\ng</^e>).
  </li>
  <li>揆：度也；
    measure \+
      揆，音葵，
      <^cm> k'wai4 | kui/ </^cm>
  </li>
====

<#t>
  如是則焉有不成者哉！ |
  {Among those} like this, how be there those who succeed not!
</#t>
