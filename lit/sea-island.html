《海島算經》 or
"The Sea Island Computational Classic"

First created: 20190420
Last modified: 20200215
Also known as Haidao Suanjing.

Introduction

《海島算經》, or "The Sea Island Computational Classic", is one of the ten canon classics of Chinese mathematics. Written by 劉徽 (Lau Fai) (Liu Hui) (Liu Hui) of the Three Kingdoms period (220–280 AD), it consists of nine problems in the surveying of distant structures or landforms. For each problem, the Classic itself only gives the statement of the problem, the answer and the formula used to compute the answer. Derivations of the formulae I found in 《海島算經細草圖說》, or "The Sea Island Computational Classic with detailed working, diagrams and explanations". Written by 李潢 (Lei Wong) (Li Huang) (Li Huang), the (arithmetic) working (草), diagrams (圖) and explanations (說) supplement the older (but at times erroneous) annotations of 李淳風 (Lei Sh_n-fung) (Li Ch'un-fêng) (Li Chunfeng). It is in the explanations (說) that I find derivations of the formulae, and they read very much like high school geometry proofs.

Here I translate the the eponymous first problem 望海島, "looking at a sea island", and its corresponding derivation as found in 《海島算經細草圖說》.

Chinese text 今有望海島 (problem) & 說曰 (derivation) taken from archive.org. After reading these in their original forms (i.e. without any punctuation) it is hard not to appreciate modern mathematical notation.

Chinese units of measurement have changed many times throughout history. These are the relevant conversions for distances at the time the Classic was written:

\begin{aligned} 3 \unit{丈} &= 5 \unit{步} \\ \unit{里} &= 300 \unit{步} \end{aligned}

The units themselves I leave untranslated, but to give you a sense of their magnitude:

  • 丈 (chœng) (chang) (zhang) is of the order two metres
  • 步 (pou) (pu) (bu) is of the order one metre
  • 里 (lei) (li) (li) is of the order half a kilometre

Problem

今有望海島，立兩表，齊高三丈，前後相去千步，令後表與前表參相直。
Now there be a looking at a sea island; erect two poles, of the same height three (3) 丈 (chœng) (chang) (zhang), one in front of the other, mutually separated by a thousand (1000) 步 (pou) (pu) (bu), and make the rear pole mutually straight the three with the front pole and the island.
  • 表：標也； poles; or pillars; lit. markers
  • 齊： the same; or uniform
  • 丈 (chœng) (chang) (zhang)
    A unit of distance, of the order two metres. Note that 3 \unit{丈} = 5 \unit{步}.
  • 前後： one in front of the other; lit. front and rear
  • 步 (pou) (pu) (bu)： lit. steps; or paces
    A unit of distance, of the order one metre.
  • 參相直： mutually straight the three; or three-in-line
    參 means 三, three (saam1/sān).
從前表卻行一百二十三步，人目著地取望島峯，與表末參合。
Walking back one hundred and twenty-three (123) 步 (pou) (pu) (bu) from the front pole, a man's eye on the ground, taking a look at the peak of the island, meeteth the three with the tip of the pole.
  • 著地： on the ground; or on ground level; lit. contacting the ground
  • 峯： peak; or summit
  • In other words, the three of them (the island's peak, the pole's tip and the person's eye) are in a straight line.
從後表卻行一百二十七步，人目着地取望島峯，亦與表末參合。
Walking back one hundred and twenty-seven (127) 步 (pou) (pu) (bu) from the rear pole, a man's eye on the ground, taking a look at the peak of the island, also meeteth the three with the tip of the pole.
問島高及去表各幾何？
We ask: how much are each of the height of the island and its distance from the front pole?
答曰：島高四里五十五步；去表一百二里一百五十步。
Answer saith: the height of the island is four (4) 里 (lei) (li) (li) fifty-five (55) 步 (pou) (pu) (bu), and the distance from the front pole, one hundred and two (102) 里 (lei) (li) (li) one hundred and fifty (150) 步 (pou) (pu) (bu).
  • 里 (lei) (li) (li)
    A unit of distance, of the order half a kilometre.
  • 一百二： one hundred and two (102)
    (Remember I distinguish words not in the original Chinese.)
    • In modern Chinese, one must say 一百零二, "one hundred oh two", to mean 102. If one just says 一百二 or 百二, this means 120; the 二 refers to the next significant digit (as in 1.2 \times 100 = 120).
    • In Literary Chinese you might see 一百又二 or 一百有二, "one hundred and two", but the "and" is not necessary. Thus 一百二 or 百二 means 102 rather than 120; this is preserved in modern Japanese.
術曰：以表高乘表間爲實；相多爲法，除之。所得加表高，即得島高。
Method saith: use the pole height times the pole separation as the dividend, and the mutual excess as the divisor, and divide them. Unto that obtained add the pole height, that is we obtain the height of the island.
  • 實： dividend; or numerator
  • 間： separation
    間，去聲， Cantonese: kaan3, Mandarin: jiàn
  • 相多： mutual excess; or difference
    It is not immediately obvious, but "mutual excess" refers to the difference between the backward walking distances from the two poles.
  • 法： divisor; or denominator
    \frac{\text{實}}{\text{法}} = \frac{\text{[dividend]}}{\text{[divisor]}}
    實 and 法 are very much mathematical jargon terms of Literary Chinese. In no other context do they mean "dividend" and "divisor".
  • In modern notation,
    \text{島高} = \frac{\text{表高} \cdot \text{表間}}{\text{相多}} + \text{表高}
    or
    \begin{aligned} \text{[island height]} &= \frac{ \text{[pole height]} \cdot \text{[pole separation]} }{ \text{[mutual excess]} } + \text{[pole height]} \\[2ex] &= \frac{ 3 \unit{丈} \cdot 1000 \unit{步} }{ 127 \unit{步} - 123 \unit{步} } + 5 \unit{步} \\[2ex] &= \frac{ 5 \unit{步} \cdot 1000 \unit{步} }{ 4 \unit{步} } + 5 \unit{步} \\[2ex] &= 1255 \unit{步} \\ &= 4 \cdot 300 \unit{步} + 55 \unit{步} \\ &= 4 \unit{里} + 55 \unit{步}. \end{aligned}
求前表去島遠近者：以前表卻行乘表間爲實；相多爲法。除之，得島去表里數。
Those seeking the distance that the front pole is separated from the island: use the front pole backward walk times the pole separation as the dividend, and the mutual excess as the divisor. Divide them, to obtain the number of 里 (lei) (li) (li) that the island is separated from the front pole.
  • 遠近： distance; lit. far-closeness
  • In modern notation,
    \text{島去表} = \frac{\text{前表卻行} \cdot \text{表間}}{\text{相多}}
    or
    \begin{aligned} \text{[island-pole separation]} &= \frac{ \text{[front pole backward walk]} \cdot \text{[pole separation]} }{ \text{[mutual excess]} } \\[2ex] &= \frac{ 123 \unit{步} \cdot 1000 \unit{步} }{ 127 \unit{步} - 123 \unit{步} } \\[2ex] &= \frac{ 123000 \unit{步}^2 }{ 4 \unit{步} } \\[2ex] &= 30750 \unit{步} \\ &= 102 \cdot 300 \unit{步} + 150 \unit{步} \\ &= 102 \unit{里} + 150 \unit{步}. \end{aligned}

Derivation

The derivation in 《海島算經細草圖說》, called an explanation (說), comes with a diagram (圖) which I have reproduced here, minus some irrelevant vertical lines. I have mapped the names of the vertices as follows:

甲 乙 丙 丁 戊 己 庚 辛 壬 辰
A B C D E F G H I O

The punctuation inserted in this section is my own.

說曰：甲乙爲海島，甲爲島峯。
Explanation saith: AB is the sea island; A is the peak of the island.
丙丁爲前表，戊丁爲前去表，戊爲人目，戊丙甲爲前表望島峯。
CD is the front pole; ED is the front walk's separation from the pole; E is the man's eye; ECA is the front pole's looking at the peak of the island.
己庚爲後表，辛庚爲後去表，辛爲人目，辛己甲爲後表望島峯。
FG is the rear pole; HG is the rear walk's separation from the pole; H is the man's eye; HFA is the rear pole's looking at the peak of the island.
與庚戊丁乙平行作己丙辰，則辰乙與丙丁、己庚俱等。
Constructing FCO parallel with GEDB, OB is equal with both CD and FG.
  • 俱： both; lit. all
與丙戊平行作己壬，則壬庚與戊丁等。
Constructing FI parallel with CE, IG is equal with ED.
己庚辛形，與甲辰己形同式。
The shape FGH is similar with the shape AOF.
  • 同式： similar; lit. same form; or same style
己庚壬形，與甲辰丙形同式。
The shape FGI is similar with the shape AOC.
己庚辛形，分己庚壬形，同己庚句，而辛壬爲股較。
The shape FGH is split into the shape FGI of the same minor cathetus FG, with HI as the major cathetus chariot-beam.
  • 句： minor cathetus; or shorter leg (of a right-angled triangle)
    Variant of 勾.
    A cathetus or leg is a non-hypotenuse side of a right-angled triangle.
  • 股： major cathetus; or longer leg (of a right-angled triangle)
    Surveying the sea island from distances much greater than its height, the horizontal lengths will be much greater than the vertical lengths. Thus the relevant horizontal segments are referred to as major catheti (股) and the relevant vertical segments as minor catheti (句).
  • 較： (horizontal) chariot-beam
    較，入聲，音覺， Cantonese: kok8, Mandarin: jiào
    If I understand the entry in 《康熙字典》 correctly, 較 are horizontal beams of a chariot which stick out like horns, above 輢 (the wooden vertical side boards) and above 軾 (which are the horizontal handrails for standing passengers). So 辛壬爲股較 is a very literary way of saying "HI sticks out horizontally" (from FGI).
甲辰己形，分甲辰丙形，同甲辰句，而己丙爲股較。
The shape AOF is split into the shape AOC of the same minor cathetus AO, with FC as the major cathetus chariot-beam.
以小股較辛壬比小句己庚，若大股較己丙比大句甲辰。
Using the small major cathetus chariot-beam HI to compare unto the small minor cathetus FG, is like the large major cathetus chariot-beam FC compared unto the large minor cathetus AO.
  • In modern notation,
    \begin{aligned} \frac{\colb HI}{\colr FG} &= \frac{\colg FC}{\colv AO} \\[2ex] {\colv AO} &= \frac{ {\colr FG} \cdot {\colg FC} }{ \colb HI }. \end{aligned}
既得甲辰，加表高辰乙（即丙丁），得甲乙，爲島高。
Having obtained AO, add the pole height OB (which is CD), to obtain AB, being the height of the island.
  • In modern notation,
    \begin{aligned} AB &= {\colv AO} + {\colr OB} \\[1ex] &= \frac{ {\colr FG} \cdot {\colg FC} }{ \colb HI } + {\colr OB} \\[2ex] \text{[island height]} &= \frac{ \text{\colr [pole height]} \cdot \text{\colg [pole separation]} }{ \text{\colb [mutual excess]} } + \text{\colr [pole height]}. \end{aligned}
    Remember
    \begin{aligned} \text{\colb [mutual excess]} &= {\colb HI} \\ &= HG - IG \\ &= HG - ED \\ &= \text{[rear pole backward walk]} - \text{[front pole backward walk]}. \end{aligned}
小股較辛壬比分股壬庚，若大股較己丙比分股丙辰（即丁乙），得島去前表之遠。
The small major cathetus chariot-beam HI compared unto the split major cathetus IG, is like the large major cathetus chariot-beam FC compared unto the split major cathetus CO (which is DB); we obtain the distance that the island is separated from the front pole.
  • In modern notation,
    \begin{aligned} \frac{\colb HI}{\colr IG} &= \frac{\colg FC}{\colv CO} \\[2ex] &= \frac{\colg FC}{\colv DB} \\[2ex] {\colv DB} &= \frac{ {\colr IG} \cdot {\colg FC} }{ \colb HI } \\[2ex] \text{\colv [island-pole separation]} &= \frac{ \text{\colr [front pole backward walk]} \cdot \text{\colg [pole separation]} }{ \text{\colb [mutual excess]} }. \end{aligned}
